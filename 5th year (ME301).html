ME301 - Heat and Flow 3

×

Summary

Heat and Flow 3 was a 20-credit class and was examined over two semesters. Semester one assessed fluid mechanics, including boundary layers, compressibility and shock waves. Whilst semester two focussed on thermodynamics, including heat transfer by conduction, convection and radiation. The class examined both semesters equally by including an exam in semester one worth 50% and the same in semester two.

Exam 2 (50%)





Overall, the class was seen as challenging but expected of a level 3 class. The material provided for the class was seen as comprehensive and aided preparation for the exams. The knowledge gained from previous H&F classes was fundamental to achieving this understanding.

Difficulty: 9
Overall Satisfaction: 6.5

- "I find Heat and flow difficult towards the end of the semester, the content always increases"

- "Expected step up in difficulty from previous heat and flow classes"

- "Second semester difficult was higher than first semester"

- "Time consuming but rewarding content with commitment to the course."

 Overall students found the class difficulty similar to previous heat and flow classes. There was an acceptable increase in difficulty for this stage of the course. As usual with Heat and Flow there is a lot of content, some of which can be challenging upon first reading, however with enough time committed to understanding the content the class is manageable.

- "2 exams at the end of each semester"

- "More effort required than expected although the exams were relevant and closely related to teaching material."

Assessment was found to be acceptable by the majority of students. An exam at the end of each semester gave consistent examination with highly relevant questions and in a format that was familiar to students.

- "Transferable skills developed such as being comfortable using data tables. This is particularly useful for further Heat and Flow classes which often use the same or similar data tables to the ones used this year."

- "Heat and flow 3 was definitely relevant to other subjects and compatible with the other classes we had."

- "Provided a good understanding of using charts, also for the heating and cooling processes which are relevant in a lot of engineering problems. Assumed knowledge was not an issue."

Highly transferable skills developed which will be of further use in future heat and flow classes and to overall expand upon students general engineering skill set.

- "Lecture slides were comprehensive and useful throughout"

- "Information in lectures was high but not overkill."

- "No extra software skills were required"

- "Plenty of worked examples which proved very helpful for learning the course content and preparing for the exam."

The material was deemed sufficient in terms of volume and quality by most students. The worked examples and tutorials proved crucial learning material for preparation for the final exam.

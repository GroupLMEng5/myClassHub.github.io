16232 - Engineering Mechanics 2

×

Summary

This class follows on from Engineering Mechanics 1 to introduce more advanced dynamics problems for solid bodies and simple mechanisms, then introduces the basics of structural and stress analysis of bending beams, shear force, bending moment diagrams and 2D stress and strain. The 20 credit, two semester class was assessed in hands-in labs and two end of semester exams.

Overall the students found the class not too difficult to grasp with a little work, where the quality of tutorials and examples reduced the difficulty by providing excellent preparation for the exam. The labs were closely related to the course material and the knowledge gained from Year 1 helped greatly in their understanding of the course. The knowledge gained in this module was also said to be extremely helpful moving forward into other related classes later in the degree.

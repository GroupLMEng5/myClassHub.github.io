Summary

Mathematics 1M gave a full overview of the mathematical concepts that are most essential to a mechanical engineer and those that are a core part of the calculation-based classes throughout the degree. The class was run over both semesters and assessed through the completion of 2 class tests, one each semester. Should a high enough grade be achieved from these assessments, exemption from the final exam was available. It is a 10 credit class.

Class Test 1 (50%)

Class Test 2 (50%)

*



*If students obtain an average of more than 60% in these class tests, they will be exempt from a final exam and their overall mark will be their average of the two class tests. If not, they will be required to complete a final exam in the May diet, and their overall mark will be the result of this exam.



Students overall found the class to be of a very good standard with a reasonable level of difficulty and a high teaching standard. Students who had taken advanced higher maths found a large overlap in topics. Despite this, it was highlighted that a higher degree of comprehension on these topics can only benefit the student throughout the degree as these skills are fundamental and are repeatedly required and tested.

 Most students found this class of a fair standard of difficulty. Those students who had taken Advanced Higher Maths at school found that there was a significant overlap in the topics covered and therefore handled it better than those who hadn’t. Students who had studied A-Level maths found the same overlap but with the topics covered in much greater detail and often with a differing approach or question strategy.

- "I liked the option of not having to do the final year exam if you scored high on the class tests. This made me do more revision on the class tests. I think there was a good amount of tests."

- "Regular assessment was useful to keep learning going, and allowed exemption from the final exam which is always sweet

 Students generally were very pleased with the assessment, consisting of four class tests throughout the year. This regular assessment assisted with learning the content and encouraged students to not let themselves fall behind with the class workload. The option to be exempt from the final exam should a high enough grade be achieved in these tests was available and provided additional motivation.

- "This class has been very helpful in other subjects. It taught the basics of mathematics, which could then be applied in classes such as Mechanics and Heat and Flow."

- "Maths is used in almost every subject, it has been very helpful across all areas"

- "The skills we learned in this class helped a lot with a couple of my other modules, particularly MATLAB (matrices) and the kinematics part of the course. I felt as though I had a general understanding of the topics covered, and was able to further develop my knowledge and skill set when dealing with these problems."

- "Maths is the basis of all the classes, so it is very necessary in all applications."

 This class was found to be extremely relevant by all students. The majority of all classes studied throughout the degree will involve some element of mathematics. This high degree of continuity provided another motivating factor to students as full comprehension of the content from this class would prove fundamental to success throughout the degree.

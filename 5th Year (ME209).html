ME209 - Mathematical Modelling and Analysis

×

Summary

This is a merging of a maths module and an engineering analysis module; the mathematics section aims to give the knowledge required for the following analysis techniques to be learned, such as differential equations, simultaneous equations and their importance in mathematical models used in engineering formulations. This is a 2 semester class which is assessed in a 50% exam at the end of semester 1 based on the mathematics section, and then 2 class tests and 2 courseworks with a total of 50% between them in semester 2, assessing the engineering analysis section of the course. Software such as Excel and MATLab were used in courseworks.

Exam (50%)

Overall, the difficulty of the class was said to be fair although it was a challenge for some to grasp using MATLab, the maths section of the class was said to be more straightforward. However, the material given for section 1 was more well received than section 2, although given that this was the purely mathematical side of the class, and the other was assessed very differently, this was said to have been okay. Doing well in the maths exam was said to have helped with tackling the second part of the class, and the assignments provided ample opportunity for a high grade. The previous maths knowledge gained helped students greatly and moving forward the skills gained in MATLab and in mathematical problems assisted in many other classes.

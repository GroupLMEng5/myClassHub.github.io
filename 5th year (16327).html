16327 - Structural Mechanics

×

Summary

Structural Mechanics was a 10-credit class which ran during semester 1. The class was examined via two class tests, weighted equally at 50%. These examinations tested the two learning outcomes of the class - solid mechanics (including 2D stress and strain, elastic constitutive relations, and yield criteria) and structural mechanics (analysis of determinate and indeterminate beams, instability, buckling, and design analysis using standards).

Class Test 1 (50%)

Class Test 2 (50%)





Previous students found the content demanding but manageable. The class tutorials were seen as very useful and included content examined in the class tests. The responses suggested that the class were also provided with many opportunities for help with the subject. The class tests were scored out of 40, however, the marking scheme allowed for marks of 25 or above to be awarded with 100%. This module provided essential knowledge for other courseworks based around structural mechanics and provided the fundamental background for classes in later years of the course.

Difficulty: 7
Overall Satisfaction: 6.8

- "Challenging, but not impossible. lecture slides were good, lecturer was clear but content was tricky."

- "Tutorials were reasonable and exam appropriate. they weren't compulsory. they reflected well on the class content."

- "The new and previously unseen nature of the content is what provided the most challenging aspect of the class."

Overall the students found this class to be difficult due to some new concepts being introduced, however felt that if adequate work was put in throughout the semester it was possible to do well.

-"There were 2 class tests, no coursework."

- "A fair amount of time would be put in to get the best marks"

- "The class test was consistent with the class content and it helped with revision."

Students found that only having two tests at the end of each semester meant lead to a lot of work for the class being condensed into the ends of each semester. Keeping up to date with tutorials throughout the semester was a piece of advice that was suggested for this class.

- "Much of the theory learned is used again later in the course"

- "Relevant to design classes"

- "It provided a useful understanding for other coursework which involved structural mechanics."

Students found that a lot of the concepts that are introduced and explored throughout this class are useful throughout the rest of the course. Therefore emphasis would be put on fully understanding the concepts fully as it will be benifitial in later years.

- "Past papers and solutions were provided. Tutorials were useful and consistent with class content."

- "Practical examples/experiments were carried out in class which added another dimension to lectures and provided a good tool for visualising some of the theory in the class."

Overall, students were happy with that offered resources - indicating that it aided their learning well.

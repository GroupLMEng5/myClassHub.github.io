ME203 - Heat and Flow 2

×

Summary

Heat and flow 2 builds on the fundamentals of fluid dynamics and thermodynamics learned in 1st year; specifically looking at further elements of the conservation of mass, momentum and energy and flow in pipes in fluid dynamics section, and then looking at the thermodynamics basics in a number of gas and liquid-vapour power cycles used in the real world. The 20 credit two semester class is assessed in two 40% exams at the end of the resective semesters, as well as 2 online quizzes, worth 10% and 5%, and a lab assessed in a report worth 5%.

Overall students felt that the class was a considerable step up from Heat and Flow 1 but the difficulty could be combatted through putting in the time to learn the subject. This was aided in the material available, the tutorial/exam solutions and lecture material presented on myplace helped in the learning process, as well as the knowledge gained in Heat and Flow 1. The assessment structure was deemed to be a strong point of the class; students were encouraged to keep on top of their studies for this class through the continuous assessment in labs, class tests and exams.
